Golden Mount

Climb Before the City Wakes

Wat Saket's golden chedi crowns an artificial hill built in the early 1800s, reached by a spiral stair lined with wind chimes and memorial plaques. Arrive at sunrise for the clearest views across Old Bangkok, or return after dark when the stupa is lit gold against the sky.

Open Daily, 7:30 AM – 7:00 PM 344 Steps to the Summit Best at Sunrise or Dusk
Old Town Bangkok

Cultural Heritage

Old Town, On Foot

The Chedee sits in Ban Baat, a quiet artisan quarter between the Golden Mount and the old city walls — everything below is reachable without a taxi.

Eat Like a Local

Bangkok's Oldest Street Food, Around the Corner

Nang Loeng Market has fed this neighbourhood for over a century — noodle stalls and Thai dessert vendors who've held the same corner for decades. Thanon Tanao's food alleys and the Old Town's night markets are a short walk the other way.

Nang Loeng Market

Bangkok's oldest fresh market, still serving the noodle and curry recipes it opened with in 1899.

Thanon Tanao Food Alley

A dense strip of stalls between the Golden Mount and Khao San, busiest after sundown.

Old Town Dessert Vendors

Coconut ice cream, khanom buang, and mango sticky rice from carts that have outlived the buildings around them.
